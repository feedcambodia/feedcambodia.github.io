Yoeut Sean

Yoeut is our liaison in Cambodia responsible for aid purchasing and distribution. Under the Khmer Rouge regime he was forced into hard labor building a dam. After that, while struggling to make a living at the Thai Cambodian border, he stepped on a land mine. He did not lose any limbs, but sustained life-threatening injuries to his torso. He still has shrapnel in his body to this day. Because he left the Thai refugee camp to find work, he was unable to obtain sponsorship to the USA. In 1990 he was able to move back to his hometown in Cambodia and resume his livelihood as a rice farmer.
